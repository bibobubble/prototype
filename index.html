Research gap

The technical problem and the behavioural problem are connected

Technical gap

Merton-style models rely on asset volatility. In practice this is often estimated from historical market data, which can lag forward-looking risk information revealed during earnings calls.

  • Historical sigma is backward-looking.
  • Management tone may reveal uncertainty earlier.
  • Tone cannot directly replace volatility.

Behavioural gap

Analysts may underweight qualitative warning signs because they are hard to quantify, compare, trust, and justify in formal credit workflows.

  • Mixed narratives increase cognitive load.
  • Qualitative tone lacks traceable action rules.
  • Overstrong model labels risk automation bias.

Quantitative risk layer

Math remains in the project, but as an evidence layer

1. Volatility feasibility test

log(σt+1) = α + ρ log(σt) + β1 Disappointmentt + β2 Delightt + γ Controlst + εt

Tests whether unexpectedly negative tone adds information beyond historical volatility, separately from unexpectedly positive tone.

2. Conditional tone adjustment

σtone = σhistorical × exp(β1 × Disappointment)

Only valid if a larger panel supports disappointment as a volatility signal.

3. Merton-style default distance

DD = [ln(V / D) + (μ - 0.5σV2)T] / (σV√T) PD = N(-DD)

The dashboard can show baseline DD and, if justified, tone-adjusted DD. Otherwise tone remains a qualitative flag.

4. Literature-aligned tone split

tone_surprise = actual_tone - expected_tone disappointment = max(expected_tone - actual_tone, 0) delight = max(actual_tone - expected_tone, 0)

This adapts Druz, Wagner and Zeckhauser's residual-negativity logic to a net-positivity pilot score.

Evidence-based decision rules

B

Evaluation plan

Small scenario study to test comprehension, trust, and actionability

01 Participant profile

3-5 finance or risk-background participants.

02 Scenario task

Review Boeing after an earnings call and select the next credit-review action.

03 Measured outcomes

Comprehension, trust, limitation clarity, actionability, workflow fit.

04 Iteration

Revise evidence tags, alert label, model context, and analyst workflow path.

Simulated rehearsal findings

  • Users understood the trigger logic.
  • Trust depended on separating financial facts from tone interpretation.
  • “Red Alert” sounded too strong, so it became “Review Trigger: High”.
  • Users wanted market and volatility context beside the qualitative signal.
